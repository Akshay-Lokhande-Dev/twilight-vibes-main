Who I Am

My name is Emma Rainsford. I am a travel writer based in Seattle, Washington, and I have been a Twilight fan since I read the first novel in 2006. I was fifteen. I finished it in a single sitting on a rainy Tuesday, and I have been a little bit in love with the Pacific Northwest ever since — which, living in Seattle, works out well.

I created TwilightVibes because every time I visited Forks, La Push, or the filming locations in St. Helens and Portland, I found myself wishing there was a single, trustworthy guide that combined the fan perspective with practical travel information. Most of what existed online was either outdated, vague, or written by people who had never actually been. I had been. So I wrote it myself.

TwilightVibes launched in 2026 as the result of years of personal visits, research, and the accumulated knowledge of someone who has driven the Olympic Peninsula in November rain specifically to experience what Stephenie Meyer was describing. Every location guide on this site reflects a place I have stood in, a road I have driven, a forest I have walked through.

Places I Have Visited

🌲 Forks, Washington — multiple visits 🌊 La Push / First Beach 🎬 St. Helens, Oregon (filming locations) 🌆 Portland, Oregon 🏔️ Olympic National Park / Hoh Rain Forest 🌊 Port Angeles, Washington 🗺️ Seattle day trips to PNW locations

I attend the Forever Twilight in Forks festival most years and have been going since its early editions. I have done the self-guided location tours, stayed at the Miller Tree Inn, and eaten at the Forks Coffee Shop more times than is probably reasonable. I know which road to drive at dawn in October for the best fog. I know where the Treaty Line sign is and how long it takes to drive from St. Helens to La Push without stopping. That is the knowledge base behind every page on this site.

Why TwilightVibes

The Twilight Renaissance changed everything. When TikTok rediscovered the saga and a new generation of fans started planning pilgrimages to Forks, I started seeing the same questions over and over: Is it worth visiting? Where exactly were the scenes filmed? What is the best time of year to go? How do I plan a road trip that covers everything?

TwilightVibes exists to answer those questions definitively, from someone who has actually done it. Not a travel aggregator. Not a content farm. A person who has stood in the rain in Forks, Washington, because that is exactly what the place demands of you.

Editorial Standards

Everything published on TwilightVibes is written or edited by me. When I cover travel information — opening hours, accommodation, festival dates — I verify it directly with the relevant venues and organisations before publishing. When I cover the lore, the cast, or the Netflix series, I link to primary sources and update pages when new information emerges.

I use affiliate links in some recommendations (books, merchandise, accommodation). These are always disclosed. The affiliate relationship never influences what I recommend — I only link to things I would genuinely suggest to a friend planning their first trip to Forks.

TwilightVibes has no official affiliation with Lionsgate, Summit Entertainment, Stephenie Meyer, or the Quileute Nation. This is an independent fan site created out of love for the story and the landscape that inspired it.
