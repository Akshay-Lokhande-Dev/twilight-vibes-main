Where Is the Cullen House in Real Life?

The Cullen house is one of the most iconic fictional homes in modern popular culture: a vast, modern glass-and-concrete structure hidden deep in the Washington rainforest, built by a family of vampires who have lived there for decades without ageing. The house represents everything about the Cullen family — their wealth, their taste, their deliberate separation from ordinary human life, their attempt to exist on the edge of a world that would destroy them if it knew they existed.

So where is it in real life? The answer is more interesting than a single address. Different real-world locations were used for the Cullen house across the five films, and the architecture of the house itself evolved significantly between the first film and the later saga. Here is the complete guide to every real Cullen house location.

1. The Original — Hoke Road House, Portland Oregon

🎬 Twilight (2008)

The house used as the Cullen residence in the first film was a spectacular privately-owned modern home on Hoke Road in the West Hills of Portland, Oregon. The house — designed by architect Robert Harvey Oshatz — is a remarkable piece of Pacific Northwest modernist architecture: all angles, glass, steel, and natural wood, cantilevered dramatically over a heavily forested slope with the trees pressing against every window.

It was the perfect Cullen house — not because it was dressed up to look like one, but because it genuinely looked like a structure that a centuries-old family with perfect taste and unlimited resources would build in the depths of a Washington rainforest. The location scouts found it in Portland and it was used for the majority of interior and exterior Cullen house scenes in the first film.

The house is a private residence. It is not open to visitors and is not on a public road. However, it has become a well-known architectural landmark and various Twilight fan tour companies have included it on driving tours of the Portland-area filming locations over the years.

📍 Hoke Road Area, Portland

2. The Forks Version — Miller Tree Inn

🏡 In Forks, Washington

While the actual filming was done in Portland and Vancouver, the closest real-world equivalent of the Cullen house that you can actually visit — and stay in — is the Miller Tree Inn in Forks, Washington. This beautiful Bed and Breakfast is a large, gracefully proportioned craftsman home surrounded by old-growth trees on a quiet residential street in Forks.

It is not a modernist glass structure like the Hoke Road house, but its setting — deep within towering old Douglas firs, with almost no visible sky through the canopy, approached along a quiet wooded lane — captures the spirit of the Cullen house location perfectly. The innkeepers lean fully into the Twilight connection and many fans choose to stay here specifically for the Cullen house experience.

📍 Visit Miller Tree Inn

3. The Breaking Dawn Houses

🎬 Breaking Dawn Part 1 & 2 (2011–12)

By the time production reached the Breaking Dawn films, much of the Cullen house interior work was done on purpose-built sets in Vancouver. However, exterior shots used a striking modernist home in the Vancouver, British Columbia area — a location that fit the evolved, grander vision of the Cullen residence that the later films required. The house in Breaking Dawn is larger and more elaborate than the original, reflecting both the increased budget of the later films and the expansion of the Cullen family story.

What Does the Cullen House Look Like?

Stephenie Meyer's description in the novel is detailed and specific: the house is three stories, completely white, with a flat roof and enormous windows that let the forest light in from every direction. It has been completely rebuilt and modified countless times over the decades, always in the most modern architectural style, always deliberately stunning. There are no curtains. There is no need for privacy from ordinary humans — no ordinary human would ever find their way to a house this deliberately hidden.

In the first film, the production design team worked with the actual Hoke Road architecture and added period-specific Cullen family details: a grand piano in the main room, Dr. Cullen's medical office, the artwork collected over centuries. The house reads simultaneously as a family home and a fortress — a place of extraordinary beauty that also keeps the world at a very specific distance.

The Cullen House Design — Architecture Deep Dive

The Hoke Road house that served as the first film's Cullen residence was designed by Robert Harvey Oshatz, a Portland architect known for organic, nature-integrated residential design. Oshatz's philosophy centres on creating buildings that breathe with their landscape rather than imposing on it — structures where the boundary between interior and forest exterior is deliberately blurred.

This made it the ideal Cullen house. The Cullens, after all, are creatures of the forest — faster, more powerful, more at home in the trees than any human. A house that seems to grow from the hillside, that has forest on every side and glass for walls, perfectly expresses a family that has chosen to live in the wilderness while maintaining extraordinary human aesthetic taste.

Can You Visit the Cullen House?

The Hoke Road house in Portland is a private residence and is not open to visitors. Do not attempt to access the property. Several Twilight-themed tour companies have offered driving tours past the general area in the past — search for current Portland Twilight tours if you want a guided experience.

For the closest genuine experience to visiting the Cullen house, the options are:

  • Stay at the Miller Tree Inn, Forks — the town's own Cullen house equivalent, bookable as a B&B.
  • Visit the Cullen House page on this portal for the full interactive experience.
  • Explore the Hoh Rainforest near Forks — the old-growth temperate rainforest is the closest real-world equivalent of the forest setting that surrounds the Cullen house in the books and films.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where exactly is the Cullen house from Twilight?

The main Cullen house used in the first Twilight film (2008) was located on Hoke Road in the West Hills of Portland, Oregon — a private modernist residence designed by architect Robert Harvey Oshatz. It is a private home and not accessible to visitors.

Is the Cullen house a real house you can visit?

The Hoke Road house used in the first film is a private residence. The Miller Tree Inn in Forks, Washington is the closest bookable accommodation that captures the spirit of the Cullen house — surrounded by old-growth forest and embraced by the Forks Twilight community.

Was the same Cullen house used in all five Twilight films?

No — different locations and sets were used across the five films. The most iconic Cullen house (the one most fans picture) is the Portland Hoke Road house from the first film. Later films used Vancouver-area locations and purpose-built sets as the production moved primarily to British Columbia.

What architect designed the real Cullen house?

The Hoke Road house was designed by Portland architect Robert Harvey Oshatz, known for his organic, nature-integrated residential designs. His philosophy of blurring the boundary between interior space and natural landscape made the house a perfect fit for the Cullen family's character.

Is there a Cullen house in Forks Washington?

There is no official Cullen house in Forks — the films were shot in Oregon and British Columbia. However, the Miller Tree Inn in Forks is the town's beloved equivalent: a beautiful, heavily wooded property that the community has embraced as the local Cullen house, and it is available as a Bed and Breakfast.
