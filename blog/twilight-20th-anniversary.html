What Happened for the 20th Anniversary

September 2025 marked 20 years since Stephenie Meyer published the original Twilight novel in 2005. What followed was a cascade of celebrations that confirmed what fans already knew: the Twilight saga was never going away. It was just getting started again.

Meyer celebrated the milestone by releasing a hardcover deluxe collector's edition of the complete saga series, with recreated cover art reimagining key moments from each book. Forks, Washington — the small, perpetually overcast town at the heart of the story — saw a surge in tourism that local businesses described as their biggest year since the films ended in 2012.

The Special Edition Collector's Book

For the 20th anniversary, Stephenie Meyer released a deluxe collectors' hardcover republishing of the saga. The special edition included a single hardcover Twilight volume plus a complete series set, each book featuring new cover art recreating iconic moments from the novels. The editions sold out quickly and became collector's items within weeks of release.

Where to find them: The special anniversary editions are available through major retailers including Amazon and Barnes & Noble, though availability fluctuates. Check your local independent bookstore — many stocked them specifically for the anniversary.

Theater Re-Release: Fathom Events (Oct–Nov 2025)

Lionsgate and Fathom Entertainment marked the 20th anniversary of the film franchise's announcement by re-releasing all five Twilight Saga films in theaters for five days — October 29 through November 2, 2025. The theatrical event was held across hundreds of cinemas in the United States and selected international markets.

The screenings were designed as fan events — many attendees came in character, in Edward/Bella/Jacob costumes, or sporting the signature pale foundation and golden contacts of the Cullen aesthetic. Social media was flooded with photos of packed theaters and emotional audience reactions from fans seeing the films on the big screen for the first time in over a decade.

The HBO Max Effect: January 2026

Just as the anniversary excitement was settling, Lionsgate announced the entire Twilight Saga would move to HBO Max on January 1, 2026. The results were immediate: three of the five films occupied the Top 10 Movies in the United States chart within days, with the original Twilight hitting #2. The saga was simultaneously dominating in the UK, Germany, France, and Australia.

The Real Cultural Impact of Twilight

It's easy to dismiss Twilight as a teen romance phenomenon. But two decades of staying power demands a more honest assessment of what the saga actually did to culture.

It Revived Vampire Fiction Worldwide

Before Twilight, the vampire genre was in post-Buffy quiet. Meyer's reimagining — romantic, high-school set, emotionally focused on the human/vampire relationship rather than horror — opened a new category that spawned dozens of imitators and ultimately influenced how supernatural YA fiction is written globally today.

It Created a New Kind of Fan Tourism

Forks, Washington, a logging town of 3,500 people, transformed itself around Twilight tourism in a way that became a case study in small-town brand building. Today, fans from Japan, Germany, Brazil, the UK, and Australia make the pilgrimage to Forks specifically because of the books and films. The phenomenon is documented, studied, and imitated.

It Launched Three Major Hollywood Careers

Kristen Stewart, Robert Pattinson, and Taylor Lautner were all relative unknowns before Twilight. Stewart went on to earn an Oscar nomination for Spencer. Pattinson starred in Christopher Nolan's Tenet and became The Batman. Lautner stepped back from Hollywood but retains a massive global fanbase. The franchise made them, and the cultural weight of those performances still defines how the world sees them.

The Daily Telegraph Called It "The Spiritual Successor to Harry Potter"

That comparison was not made lightly. The Twilight series achieved something similar to the Potter phenomenon: crossover appeal across age groups, a passionate global fandom, and a cultural footprint that extended far beyond the books into film, merchandise, tourism, music, and fashion.

"Stephenie Meyer has achieved incredible success across all English-speaking nations and Europe, and many will say that her Twilight series has filled the hole left by Harry Potter." — The Daily Telegraph

The Numbers Behind the Saga

Twenty years of Twilight means twenty years of data. Here's what the saga actually achieved, by the numbers:

  • $3.36 billion in worldwide box office across five films (2008–2012)
  • 160+ million books sold in 38 languages globally
  • #1 bestseller in 2020 for Midnight Sun, 15 years after the original novel
  • Top 10 streaming on HBO Max within days of arriving on January 1, 2026
  • 20th anniversary editions sold out in under 48 hours at major retailers
  • Forks, WA tourism — from virtually zero in 2004 to one of the most-visited small towns in the Pacific Northwest

What Forks, Washington Looks Like Today

Forks has fully embraced its Twilight identity. The town hosts the annual Forever Twilight in Forks festival every September, timed to Bella Swan's birthday (September 13). Shops carry Twilight merchandise. Local diners serve themed menus. The Forks Visitor Center dedicates significant space to Twilight-related displays.

Swan House — the filming location used as Charlie Swan's home in St. Helens, Oregon — is now a fully operational vacation rental booked months in advance by fans who want to sleep in "Bella's Room." La Push Beach draws Twilight fans year-round from across Europe, Asia, and South America.

Twenty years in, Twilight is not a relic. It's an ever-evolving cultural institution — and with the Netflix animated series in development and a potential theatrical reboot still in discussion at Lionsgate, the next twenty years may be even more interesting.
