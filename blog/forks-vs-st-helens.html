The Confusion — And Why It Exists

One of the most common questions Twilight fans ask before visiting the Pacific Northwest is: where was it actually filmed? The answer surprises almost everyone. The Twilight Saga is set entirely in Forks, Washington and the surrounding Olympic Peninsula. But the majority of the first film — including Bella's house, the Port Angeles scenes, and many of the atmospheric street shots — was filmed in St. Helens, Oregon, a small historic town 30 minutes north of Portland.

This guide settles the confusion once and for all. Every major location, whether it is the book's setting or the film's actual production site, is mapped and explained. When you arrive at either place, you will know exactly what you are looking at and why it matters.

Short answer: Visit Forks for the story. Visit St. Helens and Portland for the actual filming. Both are worth your time — for completely different reasons.

Why Did They Film in Oregon, Not Washington?

Director Catherine Hardwicke has explained the decision in several interviews. The Olympic Peninsula in Washington — where the story is set — does not have the film production infrastructure required for a studio feature. There are no film labs, equipment houses, large crew accommodation blocks, or post-production facilities anywhere near Forks. The area is genuinely remote.

Portland, Oregon, by contrast, has a substantial film industry infrastructure. It was already home to numerous productions and had the support network needed for a $37 million studio production. More importantly, the landscape around Portland — the West Hills, the Tualatin Valley, the Silver Falls forest, the Columbia River towns — provided the visual Pacific Northwest atmosphere the film needed without requiring the logistical nightmare of shooting entirely in a remote corner of the Olympic Peninsula.

St. Helens was chosen specifically because its historic Victorian riverfront could convincingly double as Port Angeles — the streets, the brick buildings, the atmosphere were a close enough match that audiences would not question it.

Location Deep-Dive: Forks

Forks is the literary setting — the town Stephenie Meyer chose by opening an atlas and finding the rainiest place in the continental US. The real Forks exists exactly as described in the books: a small logging community of around 4,000 people, 120 inches of annual rainfall, ancient forest pressing in from every side. The Community Hospital has a "Reserved for Dr. Cullen" parking sign. The Police Department is exactly where you would expect Charlie Swan to work. The Chamber of Commerce has Bella's red trucks on display.

What Forks does not have is the cinematic precision of St. Helens — you are visiting the real place that inspired the story, not the set. That distinction is real and matters for how you experience both.

Location Deep-Dive: St. Helens, Oregon

St. Helens is where the cameras actually rolled for the most iconic scenes of the first film. The Swan House exterior and interior. The Port Angeles alleyway where Edward's silver Volvo appeared. The bookstore building where Bella bought her Quileute legends book. Walking the historic St. Helens riverfront, you will recognise scene after scene — the brick architecture, the street layouts, the specific quality of the light under overcast Oregon skies.

The Swan House is particularly significant: it is occasionally available as a short-term rental (search Airbnb for "Twilight Swan House St. Helens"), making it one of the few places on earth where you can literally sleep inside a Twilight film location.

What About the Cullen House?

The Cullen house — the iconic glass and timber modernist mansion — was a private residence in the West Hills of Portland, Oregon, designed by architect Robert Harvey Oshatz. It sits on a forested hillside with floor-to-ceiling glass and the forest pressing in on every side. It is not accessible to the public, but the West Hills neighbourhood gives you the landscape context.

Frequently Asked Questions

Was any of Twilight filmed in the actual Forks, Washington?

Minimal filming was done in the actual Forks — primarily some establishing location photography and limited shots of the real Forks Police Department and hospital. The vast majority of what appears on screen was filmed in Oregon and at Kalama, Washington.

Is the bookshop in Port Angeles the real filming location?

No — the bookshop scenes were filmed at a building on the St. Helens riverfront in Oregon. However, Port Book & News in Port Angeles is the real-world inspiration for the bookshop in Meyer's novel. Bella Italia in Port Angeles is also the genuine literary inspiration for the dinner restaurant, though the film scenes were not shot there.

Which should I visit — Forks or St. Helens?

Both, ideally. They offer completely different experiences: Forks gives you the atmosphere, the real town, the literary geography — the place the story actually inhabits. St. Helens gives you the cinematic precision — the set, the specific walls and streets that appear on screen. A complete Twilight pilgrimage includes both.
