The Twilight Saga was filmed across Washington State, Oregon, and British Columbia over five films from 2008 to 2012. Despite being set in Forks, Washington, the majority of filming actually took place in Oregon — primarily in Portland, St. Helens, and the Columbia River Gorge — and later in the Vancouver, BC area for the sequels.

This is the complete, comprehensive guide to every real-world location used across all five films, organised by region, with scene references, addresses, and travel tips for visiting each one.

Quick fact: Forks, Washington — where the story is set — was almost entirely NOT filmed in Forks. Director Catherine Hardwicke chose Oregon and Washington State locations based on visual fit, not story geography.

🌲 Washington State

Forks, Washington — The Story's Home

All 5 Films (Story Setting)

Forks is where the story lives, even if the cameras rarely rolled here. The real Forks police station, community hospital, and high school grounds all exist and can be visited — they were used in limited establishing photography but not for principal filming. The town's Chamber of Commerce, Bella's truck display, and Dr. Cullen's parking spot at the hospital are the primary visitor experiences.

  • Forks Community Hospital — "Reserved for Dr. Cullen" parking spot
  • Forks Police Department — Charlie Swan's workplace
  • Forks High School — exterior grounds
  • Forks Visitor Center — Bella's red truck display

La Push, Washington — First Beach

Twilight (2008)

First Beach at La Push is the real-world beach Stephenie Meyer set the Quileute legend scenes at. It was used for establishing location photography and is the canonical setting for the beach scenes in the novel, though the film's beach scenes were shot primarily in Oregon.

  • First Beach — Bella and Jacob, Quileute legends scene (novel setting)
  • Quileute Reservation — wolf pack territory
📍 First Beach Read Full Guide →
📋 Visitor Info
📍 GPS: 47.9094° N, 124.6374° W  |  La Push Rd, La Push, WA 98350
🅿️ Parking: Free parking lot directly at First Beach
🕐 Best time: Year-round. Fog and grey skies most atmospheric Oct–Mar. Summer has better weather but more crowds.

Kalama High School, Kalama WA — Forks High School

Twilight (2008)

The actual high school scenes from the first film — including Bella's arrival, the famous cafeteria scene, and the parking lot near-accident — were filmed at Kalama High School in Kalama, Washington, about 45 minutes north of Portland. The school's distinctive covered outdoor walkways and layout were perfect for the Forks High School look.

  • Bella's first day arrival and cafeteria scene
  • Parking lot — Edward stops the van from crushing Bella
  • Outdoor school corridors and common areas
  • Classroom biology scene — Edward smelling Bella
📍 Kalama High School
📋 Visitor Info
📍 GPS: 45.9934° N, 122.8317° W  |  2002 China Garden Rd, Kalama, WA 98625
🅿️ Parking: School parking lot (visit outside school hours only)
⚠️ This is an active school — do not enter the building or visit during school hours. Exterior only, from the street or car park.
💡 Tip: The iconic covered walkways that Bella walks through on her first day are clearly visible from outside.

Port Angeles, Washington

Twilight (2008) — Novel Setting

Port Angeles is the real city where the novel's pivotal scenes take place. The alleyway rescue and the dinner at Bella Italia were set here by Stephenie Meyer and inspired by real Port Angeles locations she visited while writing. The actual filming used St. Helens, Oregon for most Port Angeles scenes, but the real Bella Italia restaurant in Port Angeles is a genuine pilgrimage stop.

  • Bella Italia — real restaurant inspiring the first date scene
  • Port Book and News — bookshop Bella visits
  • Port Angeles waterfront — establishing location

🌲 Oregon

St. Helens, Oregon — The Film's Port Angeles & Swan House

Twilight (2008)

St. Helens is the single most important filming location for the first Twilight film. The town provided Bella's Swan House, the Port Angeles alleyway scene, and the bookstore exterior — the three most recognisable non-school, non-forest locations in the film. The entire historic riverfront district doubled as the cinematic Port Angeles.

  • Swan House (private residence) — Bella and Charlie's home, all interior and exterior scenes
  • 1st Street Alleyway — Edward rescues Bella from attackers
  • Bookstore building — Bella buys Quileute legends book
  • Columbia River waterfront — Port Angeles establishing shots
  • Historic riverfront district — various Port Angeles background scenes

Hoke Road House, Portland Oregon — The Cullen House

Twilight (2008)

The iconic Cullen house exterior and most interior scenes in the first film were shot at a spectacular modernist private residence in the West Hills of Portland — the Oshatz-designed home on Hoke Road. The house's dramatic glass walls, forest integration, and contemporary architecture made it the perfect Cullen residence.

  • Bella's first visit to the Cullen house
  • Edward playing piano — "Bella's Lullaby"
  • Bella meets the Cullen family
  • Rosalie's hostile reception scene
  • James's phone call and Bella's escape plan

Silver Falls State Park, Oregon — Forest Scenes

Twilight (2008)

Several of the deep forest scenes — including sequences where Edward moves at vampire speed through trees — were filmed in Silver Falls State Park, Oregon's largest state park. The park's old-growth forest, covered in deep moss and dramatic falls, provided the perfect PNW rainforest backdrop.

  • Edward running through forest at vampire speed
  • Forest edge scenes near the Cullen house
  • Various atmospheric forest establishing shots
📍 Silver Falls State Park
📋 Visitor Info
📍 GPS: 44.8774° N, 122.6552° W  |  20024 Silver Falls Hwy SE, Sublimity, OR 97385
💵 Entry: $5 day-use fee per vehicle (Oregon State Parks annual pass accepted)
🕐 Hours: Open daily, dawn to dusk. Visitor center hours vary seasonally.
💡 Tip: The Trail of Ten Falls (7.2 miles) passes through the most atmospheric old-growth sections used for filming. Allow 3–4 hours for the full loop.

Carver, Oregon — Meadow Scene

Twilight (2008)

The famous meadow scene — where Edward reveals himself in full sunlight and the sparkling skin effect is first shown — was filmed near Carver, Oregon, in the Clackamas River area southeast of Portland. The actual meadow was on private land and is not publicly accessible, but the Carver area gives a sense of the landscape.

  • Edward sparkles in sunlight — "This is the skin of a killer, Bella"
  • First meadow sequence — Edward and Bella's honest conversation
  • Athletic display — Edward jumping, climbing, running in trees

🎬 New Moon, Eclipse & Breaking Dawn

Vancouver & British Columbia — Films 2 through 5

New Moon (2009) · Eclipse (2010) · Breaking Dawn (2011–12)

From New Moon onwards, the Twilight production relocated almost entirely to Vancouver, British Columbia and the surrounding area. The lower production costs, generous tax incentives, and the Pacific Northwest landscape of the BC coast made it the obvious choice for continuing the saga. Specific filming locations in BC include:

  • Minnekhada Regional Park — forest scenes replacing Oregon locations
  • University of British Columbia — various school scenes
  • Various private estates in Greater Vancouver — Cullen house (rebuilt)
  • Squamish and Whistler areas — mountain and cliff diving scenes (New Moon)
  • Italy scenes for New Moon — actually filmed in Montepulciano, Tuscany, Italy
  • Breaking Dawn Isle Esme — filmed in Brazil (Paraty and Ilha Grande)
📍 Vancouver, BC

Montepulciano, Italy — Volterra (New Moon)

New Moon (2009)

The stunning Volturi city of Volterra was filmed in the real Italian hill town of Montepulciano in Tuscany. The ancient stone streets, the central piazza, and the dramatic medieval architecture made it a perfect stand-in for the ageless city of the Volturi. The production closed the town's famous Sant'Agostino square for filming the Saint Marcus Day festival scene.

  • Saint Marcus Day festival — the entire red-cloaked crowd sequence
  • Bella running through the streets to reach Edward in time
  • Edward stepping into the sunlight in the piazza
  • Underground Volturi lair (built on set in Vancouver)
📍 Montepulciano, Italy
📋 Visitor Info
📍 GPS (Piazza Grande): 43.0991° N, 11.7813° E  |  Piazza Grande, Montepulciano, Siena, Italy
🚗 Getting there: ~1.5 hours from Florence or Siena by car. Limited train access — rental car recommended.
🕐 Best time: April–June or September–October to avoid peak summer crowds.
💡 Tip: The Sant'Agostino square where Edward steps into the sunlight is a short walk downhill from Piazza Grande. Most fans visit both in a 2–3 hour walking tour of the historic centre.

Paraty & Ilha Grande, Brazil — Isle Esme (Breaking Dawn)

Breaking Dawn Part 1 (2011)

The honeymoon sequences on Isle Esme were filmed on location in Paraty and Ilha Grande on Brazil's Green Coast — one of the most beautiful stretches of coastline in South America. The combination of jungle-covered islands, crystal-clear water, and remote beaches created the perfect paradise for the Cullen honeymoon.

  • Isle Esme arrival and honeymoon beach scenes
  • Underwater sequences — Bella and Edward swimming at night
  • Jungle and beach location scenes
📍 Paraty, Brazil

Frequently Asked Questions

Was Twilight filmed in Forks, Washington?

Only minimally. The story is set in Forks but the first film was shot primarily in Oregon (St. Helens, Portland, Silver Falls, Carver, Vernonia) and at Kalama High School in southern Washington. The Forks locations exist and can be visited, but they are real-world story settings rather than actual film sets.

Where was the most Twilight filming done?

The first film was primarily shot in Oregon — with St. Helens providing the most iconic locations. Films 2 through 5 were primarily shot in and around Vancouver, British Columbia, with select international locations (Italy, Brazil) for specific sequences.

Which Twilight filming location is most worth visiting?

St. Helens, Oregon offers the most concentrated collection of recognisable filming locations in one walkable area: the Swan House, the alleyway, and the bookstore building are all within easy walking distance of each other. For the story atmosphere without film locations, Forks and La Push are the most evocative destinations.

Can I visit the Bella Italia restaurant from Twilight?

Yes — Bella Italia in Port Angeles, Washington is open to the public and is the real restaurant that inspired the novel's dinner scene. Make a reservation in advance. Order the mushroom ravioli.
