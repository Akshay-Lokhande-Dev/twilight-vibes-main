What Is "Vampire Weather"?

Stephenie Meyer chose Forks, Washington for one specific reason: it receives more rain than almost anywhere else in the continental United States. Around 120 inches per year. The sky is grey most of the year. The air is cool and damp. The forest presses in close from every direction. This is the weather that made Forks the perfect home for a family of vampires who cannot be seen in direct sunlight.

For Twilight fans, the best time to visit is not summer. Summer brings clear skies, warm temperatures, and direct Pacific sunlight that banishes the moody atmosphere entirely. The best time to visit Forks — for the full vampire weather experience — is the rainy season. And that means most of the year.

Month-by-Month Guide

🌫️ October — Peak Vampire Weather (Highly Recommended)

October is the sweet spot. The summer crowds are gone, the rains have returned, morning fog fills the valleys, and the light has that specific grey-blue quality that defines the Twilight visual world. The forests are at their most saturated green. Accommodation is cheaper than summer. St. Helens also hosts its Halloweentown festival throughout October — combining Twilight and Halloween atmosphere in one moody Pacific Northwest town.

🍂 September — Festival Season

The Forever Twilight in Forks festival runs in September — guided tours, cast appearances, themed events, and atmospheric evening walks through the town. If you can only visit once and want the full community experience, September is the answer. Weather is transitional — some summer clarity, some autumn moodiness. Accommodation books out months in advance; plan early.

🌧️ November–February — Maximum Atmosphere

These are the months of genuine Pacific Northwest winter — persistent rain, low cloud, brief days, and the specific solitude of a small town in the off-season. The most atmospheric photos come from this period. Hurricane Ridge may be closed for snow. Some accommodation options close. The experience is raw, real, and entirely consistent with the world Stephenie Meyer described. If atmosphere is your priority, come in winter.

☀️ June–August — Clear But Crowded

Pacific Northwest summers are genuinely beautiful — warm, clear, with long golden days. Hurricane Ridge is spectacular. La Push beaches are swimmable (cold). But the moody Twilight atmosphere largely disappears. Prices peak in July–August. Booking is essential 3+ months ahead. If this is your only available window, go — the landscape is still extraordinary. Just don't expect the grey magic.

🌸 March–May — The Shoulder Season

Spring on the Olympic Peninsula brings the highest waterfall flows, abundant wildflowers at altitude, and the return of long days. Rain is still frequent (Forks averages rain in some form almost every day March through May). Prices are moderate, crowds are light, and the combination of occasional misty days with spring green forest is genuinely beautiful. A good option if September and October don't work.

The Twilight Aesthetic Photography Calendar

If you are visiting for photographs — for content, for memories, for the specific visual language of Twilight Core — here is the photographer's calendar:

  • Best fog: October–November, early morning (6–9 AM). The valleys fill with fog as cold air from the mountains meets warm Pacific moisture. Drive the La Push Road at dawn in November.
  • Best rain shots: Any month October through April. The rain is fine and constant — it fills the air rather than falling in visible drops, which creates the specific photographic quality of Twilight landscapes.
  • Best forest light: October–November when deciduous trees add colour, or March when new growth is brilliant green against old-growth dark trunks.
  • Best beach atmosphere: October–March for full Twilight grey-sea mood. Summer beaches are beautiful but too bright and blue for the aesthetic.
  • Best mountain views: June–September only. Hurricane Ridge is frequently cloud-covered October through May.

What to Pack for Vampire Weather

🧥 Waterproof jacketEssential. Non-negotiable. Not an umbrella — locals don't use them. A proper rain jacket.
👟 Waterproof footwearTrail runners or hiking boots with waterproofing. Beach sand + Pacific rain = wet feet guaranteed.
🧣 LayersThe Peninsula is cool year-round. Mornings and evenings drop significantly even in summer.
📱 Offline mapsCell service is limited or absent between Port Angeles and La Push. Download before you leave.
📚 The BooksRead (or reread) Twilight before you go. The landscape makes the prose viscerally real in a way that surprises people.
📷 Camera / ND filterOvercast light is even and beautiful for photography. An ND filter helps with waterfall long exposures.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the worst time to visit Forks?

There is no truly bad time — the question is what experience you want. July and August bring the clearest weather and highest prices but lose the moody atmosphere. January and February give maximum atmosphere but some facilities and roads may be closed.

Does it actually rain every day in Forks?

Close to it, October through May. Forks records precipitation on over 210 days per year. The rain is typically light and persistent rather than heavy — a fine drizzle that fills the air and creates the saturated, misty quality you associate with the Twilight world. Most days you can still be outside comfortably in good rain gear.
