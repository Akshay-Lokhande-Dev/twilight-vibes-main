Your Complete Twilight Road Trip Plan

This is the itinerary that every Twilight fan deserves — a fully planned, 3-day Pacific Northwest road trip covering every major real-world location from the saga. Seattle to Port Angeles. Forks and the Swan House. La Push and the wolf pack beaches. Three days, one unforgettable journey.

This guide includes drive times, specific stops, restaurant recommendations, booking tips, and the best order to experience everything. Use it as a master plan and customise to your pace. Every city page on TwilightVibes links from here for deeper reading.

Best season: October–November for fog, moody skies, and full Twilight atmosphere. September adds the Forever Twilight in Forks festival. June–August for clear mountain views and easier driving.

Day 1

Seattle to Port Angeles — The Arrival

7:30 AM
Seattle Waterfront — Bainbridge Ferry TerminalStart at Coleman Dock in downtown Seattle. Take the 35-minute Winslow ferry to Bainbridge Island. Run every 35–50 min. Stunning Puget Sound views + Olympic Mountain backdrop.
9:30 AM
Bainbridge Island → Highway 101Drive north through Bainbridge and across the Hood Canal Bridge toward Port Gamble and Highway 101 west. 75-minute scenic drive through coastal forest.
11:00 AM
Port Angeles — Waterfront WalkArrive PA. Park at the waterfront, walk the harbour. See the Strait of Juan de Fuca — the body of water that defines the north edge of Bella's world. Stop at Port Book & News — Bella's bookshop.
1:00 PM
Hurricane Ridge — Optional Day Addition45-min drive, 5,242 feet. If weather is clear: go. Olympic Mountain panorama above the clouds. If cloudy: skip and rest for dinner.
7:00 PM
Bella Italia — DinnerThe real restaurant that inspired Edward and Bella's first date. Order the mushroom ravioli — Bella's order. Book in advance. This is the highlight of Day 1.
Night
Stay in Port AngelesHarbour-view hotel or B&B. Waking up to fog on the Strait of Juan de Fuca is the perfect start to Day 2.

Day 2

Day 3

La Push Beaches — Wolf Territory

8:00 AM
Forks → La Push (14 miles, 25 min)West on La Push Road through dense rainforest. Pass the Treaty Line sign on the way — the legendary boundary where vampire territory ends. Worth stopping for a photo.
8:30 AM
First Beach — MorningArrive early before any other visitors. Walk the full length of the beach. Sit on driftwood. Watch the sea stacks emerge from morning fog. This is Jacob's beach. This is the scene. Allow 90 minutes.
10:30 AM
Second Beach Trail (0.8 miles)Hike through old-growth cedar and Sitka spruce to the most dramatic beach on the Peninsula. Sea stacks, tidepools (check low tide tables), ancient forest. Allow 2.5 hours.
1:30 PM
Lunch — Quileute Oceanside Resort CaféEat on Quileute land, support tribal businesses. Simple, fresh, coastal.
3:00 PM
Rialto Beach + Hole-in-the-WallNorth of La Push across the Quillayute River. 1.5 mile beach walk to the natural sea arch. Check tide tables — only passable at low tide. One of the great short hikes in Washington.
6:00 PM
Return JourneyBack to Port Angeles (1.5 hrs) for onward travel or Seattle ferry (departs PA 7:10 PM for Bainbridge, arrives 8:45 PM, then 30 min to Seattle). Or stay another night.

Booking & Planning Checklist

  • Bella Italia (Port Angeles): Book 3–5 days ahead minimum. Friday/Saturday evenings fill up completely. Call or book online.
  • Bainbridge Ferry: Walk-on passengers (no car) have more flexibility. Vehicle bookings recommended in summer — book at WSdot.com.
  • Miller Tree Inn (Forks): Book 2+ months ahead. September festival dates book out 4+ months in advance.
  • Hurricane Ridge: Free with park entry. Road closes in winter — check NPS.gov for current status. Arrive before 10am in summer to avoid crowds.
  • Tide Tables: Essential for Rialto Beach Hole-in-the-Wall and Second Beach tidepools. Check tidesandcurrents.noaa.gov before you leave home.
  • Rental Car: Non-negotiable. No public transport connects these locations. Budget $50–90/day. Book in advance from Seattle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 3 days enough for a Twilight road trip?

Three days covers the essential experience — Port Angeles, Forks, and La Push — at a comfortable pace. For St. Helens (the actual filming location) and Portland (Cullen House neighbourhood, Silver Falls), allow 5 days.

Can I do this road trip from Seattle without a car?

No — the Olympic Peninsula has no public transport connecting these locations. A rental car from Seattle is essential. Budget $50–90 per day plus fuel. The Bainbridge ferry crossing can be done as a walk-on passenger, with the car rented from a provider on the Bainbridge side.

What is the Forever Twilight in Forks festival?

An annual fan festival in Forks every September — guided tours, cast appearances, themed events, and atmospheric night walks. If you can align your trip with the festival dates, do it. Book accommodation 4+ months ahead.
