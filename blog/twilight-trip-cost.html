The Real Cost of a Twilight Pilgrimage

Planning a Twilight trip to Forks, Washington is a dream for millions of fans — but how much does it actually cost? This guide breaks down every real expense for a 3-day Olympic Peninsula Twilight road trip in 2026, from flights to Seattle to the mushroom ravioli at Bella Italia. Budget, mid-range, and splurge options included.

Total estimated cost per person (3 days, mid-range): $650–$950 USD. Budget traveller: $380–$500. Comfortable splurge: $1,100–$1,400.

Getting There — Flights to Seattle

✈️ Domestic US Flight$150–$400 round tripSeattle-Tacoma (SEA). Book 4–6 weeks ahead. Avoid peak summer.
✈️ International Flight$600–$1,400 round tripUK, Europe, Australia. SEA is well-connected with direct routes.
🚌 Airport to Ferry Terminal$4–$55Light rail to downtown: $4. Taxi/rideshare: $40–55.
⛴️ Bainbridge Ferry$9–$15 (walk-on)Per person each way. Car: $23–50 extra. Most scenic option.

The Rental Car — Non-Negotiable

A rental car is absolutely essential for the Olympic Peninsula. No public transport connects Seattle, Port Angeles, Forks, and La Push. Budget $50–$90 per day. For 3 days including insurance and fees, expect $180–$300 total.

Twilight fan joke: Request a silver Volvo. Your rental company will not have one. But you can ask.
🚗 Economy Car (3 days)$150–$220Book early from Seattle airport. Enterprise, Hertz, Budget all have locations.
⛽ Fuel (3 days, ~300 miles)$45–$65Gas stations in Forks and Port Angeles. Fill up before heading to La Push.
🅿️ Parking$0–$10/dayForks and La Push are largely free. Port Angeles waterfront has paid lots.
🏔️ Olympic NP Entry$30 per vehicleValid 7 days. America the Beautiful annual pass: $80 (worth it for multiple parks).

Accommodation — 3 Nights

🏡 Miller Tree Inn, Forks$160–$220/nightThe "Cullen House" B&B experience. Book 2+ months ahead.
🏨 Forks Motel$90–$130/nightBudget option. Clean, central, functional. Good choice for cost-conscious fans.
🌊 Quileute Oceanside Resort$150–$250/nightOceanfront cabins on First Beach, La Push. Wake up in wolf territory.
🏨 Port Angeles Hotel$110–$180/nightHarbour view recommended. Book ahead for summer and festival season.

Food — The Twilight Dining Budget

🍝 Bella Italia (Port Angeles)$35–$55 per personDinner with mushroom ravioli, wine, tip. The non-negotiable splurge of the trip.
🍕 Pacific Pizza, Forks$15–$22 per personBest meal in Forks. Lunch recommendation.
☕ Coffee & Breakfasts$10–$18/dayCafé Garden in Port Angeles for views. Basic but satisfying across the Peninsula.
🛒 Groceries / Snacks$20–$40 totalStock up in Port Angeles before driving west. Forks has a small Thriftway.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Book Bella Italia early: A Friday night dinner for two is $70–100 — the most expensive meal of the trip. It is also the most worthwhile. Budget for it specifically.
  • America the Beautiful Pass: At $80 it covers Olympic National Park plus every other national park and federal recreation site. If you visit more than 2–3 national parks in a year it pays for itself immediately.
  • Walk-on ferry: If renting a car from the Bainbridge side rather than Seattle, walk-on ferry tickets are significantly cheaper than vehicle tickets.
  • Stock groceries in Port Angeles: Food options between Port Angeles and La Push are limited. Stock breakfast and lunch supplies before heading west.
  • Avoid July–August peak: Prices for accommodation and ferry bookings are 20–35% higher in peak summer. October visits are cheaper, less crowded, and far more atmospheric.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a Twilight trip to Forks worth the money?

For any genuine Twilight fan, absolutely. The Olympic Peninsula is also one of the most extraordinary landscapes in the United States entirely independent of Twilight — old-growth rainforest, wild Pacific coastline, hurricane-level mountain views. The Twilight connection makes a trip to one of America's great wild places feel personally meaningful. That is a rare combination.

Can I do this trip cheaply?

The core expenses — rental car, fuel, and at least one night's accommodation — are largely fixed. The most impactful savings come from visiting in October rather than summer (cheaper accommodation, no crowds), walking onto the Bainbridge ferry instead of taking a car, and cooking some of your own meals.
